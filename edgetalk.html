What EdgeTalk Does

EdgeTalk is a macOS voice typing tool. It runs entirely on your device — no internet, no account, no data collection. Hold right Cmd, speak, release, and the recognized text appears wherever your cursor is: Notes, browser, terminal, Messages, any app.

EdgeTalk is built for accessibility: for users who find conventional keyboards difficult to use, voice replaces the keyboard. The Accessibility permission enables this — it does nothing else.

Permissions

Microphone

Required to capture audio for speech recognition. All processing happens locally. Audio data never leaves the device.

Accessibility

Required to paste recognized text at the active cursor via Cmd+V. Enables voice typing for users with motor impairments or typing difficulties. Never reads or accesses content from other apps.

Key Features

100% Offline

All speech recognition and correction run locally on your Mac. No cloud, no account, no data collection.

Dual AI Models

Lightweight zipformer model streams text in real time. Qwen3-ASR 0.6B LLM corrects on release for higher accuracy.

Bilingual

Supports Chinese and English natively. Switch languages on the fly without changing settings.

Lightweight

~5–10% CPU, ~160MB memory. Runs quietly in your menu bar.

Requirements

macOS 14.0 or later. Apple Silicon (M1/M2/M3/M4). Microphone required.
